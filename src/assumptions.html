Kinetic Model of an Ideal gas involves utilizing statistical models - in this case, concepts such as averaging and mean values - to analyze data and draw relevant conclusions. In the process, macroscopic porperties such as pressure will be considered based on molecular movement.

The following are the 8 Key Assumptions behind the Kinetic Model:

  1. Gases consist of a large number of tiny particles called molecules
  2. The numer of molecules is large enough to make statistical averages
  3. The volume of each molecule is negligible when compared to the volume of the gas as a whole
  4. The duration of collision is negligible when compared to the interval between the collisions
  5. The collision between the molecules and the collision between the molecules and the container walls are perfectly elastic collisions
  6. There are no intermolecular forces between the molecules, so the internal energy of the gas is entirely kinetic
  7. The molecules are in constant random motion and direction
  8. Each molecule produces force against the wall, and the forces averages out as pressure
