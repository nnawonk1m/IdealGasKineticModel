The figure above shows the movement of the molecule that we are considering.

F = ma = m * Δv / Δt = Δmv / Δt = rate of change of momentum

In Part1: Momentum, we had calculated that Δp = Δmv = -2mcx. Thus, the value of Fx can be expressed as follows:

Fx = Δmv / Δt = -2mcx / t

Now, let us think about how else we can express time. We are comparing the momentum before and after the collision, so the range of movement we are observing is when the molecule starts from one end of the cube, collides with the opposite side of the wall, and arrives back at its original position. Therefore, the molecule would have travelled 2L in t seconds with the speed of cx.

t = 2L / cx

F = -2mcx / (2L / cx) = -mcx2 / L
