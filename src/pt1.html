Here, the figure above shows one of the N gas molecules present inside the container, a cube with a side length of L and a volume of V. Let us say that the mass of one molecule is m, and the velocity of each molecule is c. c is shown as a velocity vector(the pink arrow in the figure). If we were to resolve the vector c, its x, y, and z component would be expressed as cx(the red arrow), cy, and cz.

  • number of molecules: N
  • cube edge length: L
  • cube volume: V
  • mass of one molecule: m
  • velocity of molecule: c

c2 = cx2 + cy2 + cz2

When the molecule shown moves and collides with the wall, cx will be reversed while cy and cz will be conserved. This is because the molecule collides with the yz plane.

  • cx → -cx
  • cy, cz conserved

Momentum is equal to mass multiplied by velocity. Our calculations from now on will only consider the x-component of the molecule. Initially, the x-component of the momentum(p) of the molecule is m multiplied by cx. After the collision with the wall, the molecule bounces off the wall and changes its velocity - cx is reversed. Therefore after the collision the x-component of the momentum of the molecule is m multiplied by -cx.

p = mv

after collision: p = -mcx

before collision: p = mcx

Therefore, the change in momentum(Δp) is equal to -2mcx.

Δp = -mcx - (mcx) = -2mcx
