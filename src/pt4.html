P = F / A

Pressure is equal to force applied per unit area. Now that we know the total force exerted by the gas molecules on one side(L * L) of the container, we are able to calculate the pressure of the gas molecules.

Px = Fx / A = 1/3 * (Nm / L) * c2 / (L * L) = 1/3 * (Nm / L3) * c2

V = L3

Pressure at a point acts equally in all directions

Px = P = 1/3 * (Nm / V) * c2

Now, Nm is (number of molecules * mass of one molecule) = total mass of the gas molecules. Density is equal to mass per unit volume. Therefore, Nm / V = (total mass) / (volume) = density of gas molecules

Nm / V = ρ

P = 1/3 * ρc2
